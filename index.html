I am Chuan-Peng Zhang, an astronomer working at the National Astronomical Observatories, Chinese Academy of Sciences (NAOC) as an Assistant Scientist, and at the Max-Planck-Institut für Astronomie (MPIA) as a postdoc now.

Current Work

The fragmentation at the earliest phases is an important process of massive star formation. Investigations of starless cores and prestellar cores are crucial for understanding the origin of the stellar initial mass function. However, few high-angular resolution observations toward the earliest high-mass clumps have been done so far. Eight massive precluster clumps (G18.17, G18.21, G23.97N, G23.98, G23.44, G23.97S, G25.38, G25.71) were selected from the SCUBA Massive Pre/Protocluster core Survey (SCAMPS, Thompson et al. 2005) at 850 and 450 μm. VLA D-configuration at 1.3 cm, PdBI BCD-configuration at 3.5 and 1.3 mm, APEX at 870 μm observations were followed up, and archival infrared data at 4.5, 8.0, 24, and 70 μm are combined to study the fragmentation and evolution of these eight clumps. The spectral data including NH3 (1, 1) and (2, 2) lines NH2D, C18O (2-1), H13CN, HC15N, and SO line data will be used to study the dynamics, stability, and chemistry of these samples. Finally, we want to further unveil the initial conditions of high-mass star formation.

As for the highly deuterated NH2D cores, we will further to study with another tracer H2D+, because these cores are the best targets to detect the elusive H2D+. It is a tracer of cold and CO-depleted environments, and therefore reveals observationally the earliest stages of star formation. Therefore, we propose to conduct observations of ortho-H2D+ and para-H2D+ in massive quiet cores to probe the structure and dynamics, especially the evolutionary age (Brünken et al. 2014), in the innermost zone of high-mass cores with JCMT, APEX, SOFIA, and ALMA.
