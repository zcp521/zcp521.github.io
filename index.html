Current Work

Context. The Bok globule CB 26 harbours a young star, around which a protoplanetary disk is formed. Grain growth within the disk determines the following planet formation process.

Aims. The goal is to study the grain growth and physical structure in the edge-on circumstellar disk of the Bok globule CB 26.

Methods. Combining with high angular resolution millimetre observations at 1.1, 1.3, 2.9, and 8.1 mm with SMA, OVRO, and PdBI instruments, and using the well-tested radiative transfer code RADMC-3D, we conducted a detailed modelling of the dust emission from the circumstellar disk and envelope around CB 26.

Results. The best-fit model fits well with observations in the spectral energy distribution between 0.9 μm and 6.4 cm, and in the image planes at 1.3 and 2.9 mm. Assuming a constant gas-to-dust mass ratio of 100, the total gas mass of the disk and envelope is gas M(disk+env)= 0.11 Msun, which is 20% of the central star mass Mstar = 0.55 Msun. The millimetre spectral index of the data between 1.3 and 2.8 mm is α mm = 2.79 ± 0.43. Assuming in optically thin condition, the millimetre dust opacity is β mm = 0.79 ± 0.43, which is well consistent with the best-fit model of dust grain distribution with a disk max = 5 cm, a max = 110 μm, and p = −3.5. The sharp peak at 8.1 mm is mainly produced by free-free emission, while the wide wings are produced by dust emission. The entire disk with 1.9 < Q < 16.6 is gravitationally stable. However, the outer disk (r & 80 AU) is prone to collapse (1.9 < Q < 3.5) under the gravitational pull.

Conclusions. In the disk of CB 26, the dust grain particles have grown into large grains with sizes of centimeter order of magnitude.
